UARM:

Uniformly Accelerated Rectilinear Motion (UARM) is a type of motion in which an object travels along a straight line while experiencing a consistent acceleration. This implies that the object's velocity changes by a consistent amount during equal time intervals. The direction of this acceleration can be either positive or negative, depending on the object's motion direction.

UARM

The motion of an object moving in a straight line with a constant acceleration is often referred to as uniformly accelerated rectilinear motion (UARM).

Topics

"UARM"

In this type of motion, the object's displacement, velocity, and acceleration can be precisely described using mathematical formulas, commonly known as the equations of motion. These equations establish relationships between the object's initial velocity, final velocity, acceleration, displacement, and the time elapsed during the motion. The study of uniformly accelerated rectilinear motion holds great significance in physics, as it serves as a foundational concept for comprehending the principles of motion and forces.
