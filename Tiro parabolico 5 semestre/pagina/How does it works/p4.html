Law of hooke:

Hooke's Law is a fundamental principle in physics, which asserts that the force needed to extend or compress a spring is directly proportional to the distance the spring is stretched or compressed from its equilibrium position.

More Information

When?

In 1660, the English scientist Robert Hooke formulated Hooke's Law.

Why?

Hooke also observed that using springs instead of a pendulum to regulate clocks resulted in a more efficient timekeeping mechanism.,

How?

Through a series of experiments involving the application of specific weights to a spring, Hooke arrived at his observations and conclusions regarding the behavior of springs and their relationship to force and displacement..

Topics.

The law of hooke.

This relationship can be expressed mathematically as F = -Kx, where F represents the applied force, x denotes the displacement from the equilibrium position, and K represents the spring constant. Hooke's Law holds significant importance in various domains of physics and engineering, including the design of suspension systems, the study of material behavior under stress, and the operation of various instruments.
